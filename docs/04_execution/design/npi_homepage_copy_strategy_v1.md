# NPI Website — Homepage Copy Strategy v1

Purpose of this document:
Define what the homepage should communicate, section by section, so that visual redesign and implementation stay aligned with NPI's real positioning.

This is not final polished marketing copy.
This is a strategic copy framework for writing the homepage with clarity, restraint, and direction.

---

## 1. Core copy principle

The homepage must not try to say everything.

It must help the visitor:
- understand who NPI is
- understand what NPI does
- trust NPI
- identify relevant capabilities
- move toward references or contact

Every sentence on the homepage should do one of four jobs:
- explain
- reassure
- orient
- direct

If a sentence does not do one of those things, it should probably not be there.

---

## 2. Audience mindset

Primary audience:
- investors
- project owners
- domestic and foreign industrial clients
- partners
- EPC and infrastructure stakeholders

What they are likely looking for:
- a credible engineering partner
- proof of technical competence
- evidence of real project experience
- confidence that NPI understands complex project environments
- a fast path toward references or contact

What they are not looking for:
- long self-congratulatory company history
- generic “innovation” language
- broad unsupported claims
- heavy text blocks
- marketing slogans without engineering substance

---

## 3. Homepage messaging hierarchy

The homepage should communicate in this order:

### First:
Who NPI is and what kind of engineering partner it is.

### Second:
What NPI actually does.

### Third:
How NPI supports projects.

### Fourth:
Why NPI is credible.

### Fifth:
Where the visitor should go next.

That is the intended psychological flow of the page.

---

## 4. Tone of voice rules

The homepage tone should be:
- professional
- clear
- restrained
- technically credible
- internationally readable
- confident without exaggeration

The homepage tone should not be:
- salesy
- flashy
- over-enthusiastic
- vague
- stuffed with buzzwords
- internally focused

### Preferred language style
Use:
- short sentences
- direct statements
- precise engineering/business wording
- clean nouns and verbs
- compact descriptive phrases

Avoid:
- stacked adjectives
- clichés
- “leading provider of innovative...” type phrasing
- abstract corporate filler
- too many claims in one paragraph

---

## 5. Section-by-section copy strategy

## A. Header / Navigation

Purpose:
Help visitors orient immediately.

Navigation labels should stay simple:
- Home
- About
- Services
- References
- Contact

Do not overload navigation with extra items in v1.

The header should not try to sell.
It should simply orient and support access.

---

## B. Hero

### Hero job
The hero must answer:
What is NPI, in one clear impression?

### Hero must communicate
- multidisciplinary engineering competence
- relevance to industrial and infrastructure projects
- confidence and professionalism
- project-oriented support, not generic consulting

### Hero structure
- small label above title
- strong headline
- short supporting paragraph
- primary CTA
- optional short trust line

### Hero headline guidance
The headline should be:
- broad enough to cover the company
- specific enough to sound like engineering
- short enough to feel strong

Good headline direction:
- engineering support for complex industrial projects
- integrated technical solutions for demanding project environments
- multidisciplinary engineering for energy and industrial systems
- from concept to execution, with technical clarity

Avoid headline direction like:
- empowering your future
- building innovation together
- transforming businesses with solutions
- generic growth language

### Hero supporting text guidance
Supporting text should clarify:
- that NPI supports real projects
- that the company works across multiple engineering disciplines
- that the company helps from early definition through execution-ready documentation

Supporting text should not become a mini brochure.

### Hero CTA guidance
Recommended primary CTA:
- Explore References
or
- View References

Possible secondary CTA later:
- Explore Services

For now, one strong CTA is enough.

---

## C. Short About / Intro section

### Section job
Answer:
Who is behind this company, and why should I take them seriously?

### This section should communicate
- NPI is an established engineering company
- it works in relevant industrial sectors
- it brings multidisciplinary capability
- it supports projects through structured technical delivery

### This section should contain
- one clear heading
- one compact paragraph
- one proof-oriented supporting detail
- a clients/partners strip below

### Messaging direction
The paragraph should not be biographical.
It should be positioning-oriented.

Good direction:
- NPI supports industrial and infrastructure projects through coordinated engineering disciplines and practical project experience.
- The company delivers technical documentation, design support, and engineering coordination across complex project requirements.

Avoid:
- long founding story
- too many markets, sectors, and services in one paragraph
- claims without grounding

---

## D. Key Services section

### Section job
Help visitors quickly identify what NPI can do.

### Strategy
This section should be scannable in seconds.

Each service card should contain:
- service title
- one short description
- strong icon
- nothing more

### Service description rule
Each description should answer:
What kind of support does this area provide?

Not:
Why this service is amazing.

### Recommended content direction
Potential service group copy should be written around practical project support, for example:
- process and mechanical systems
- instrumentation and control
- electrical engineering
- telecom / fire & gas / safety systems
- project documentation and engineering support

These labels may later be refined to match NPI’s final positioning exactly.

### Important
Do not write long descriptions here.
One sentence per service is enough.

---

## E. How We Work / Project Lifecycle section

### Section job
Show how NPI supports a project through real stages.

This is important because it moves the visitor from:
“what you do”
to
“how you actually work”

### This section should communicate
- structured delivery
- lifecycle understanding
- practical engineering involvement
- coordination from early phase to detailed phase

### Good thematic direction
- from concept to commissioning
- from technical studies to execution-ready documentation
- engineering support across project phases

### Copy structure
- one strong heading
- one supporting paragraph
- optional short stage list or compact lifecycle summary

### Supporting paragraph should answer
How does NPI engage with projects?

It should suggest:
- concept / studies
- permitting / documentation
- FEED / detail engineering
- support during execution
- commissioning support where relevant

The section should sound operational, not promotional.

---

## F. Numbers / Facts section

### Section job
Use facts to build trust quickly.

### This section should communicate
- scale
- capacity
- continuity
- experience

### Good candidates for numbers
- departments
- engineers
- completed projects
- returning clients
- years of experience, if confirmed and useful

### Copy rule for stat labels
Stat labels should be short and factual.

Examples of good label style:
- engineering departments
- engineers
- completed projects
- returning clients

Avoid:
- overblown KPI language
- pseudo-financial performance claims
- vague words like “success rate” unless fully defensible

### Section framing
This section can be introduced with a heading like:
- NPI in numbers
- Neopetrol through numbers

The intro text, if any, should be minimal.

---

## G. Featured References section

### Section job
Turn credibility into concrete evidence.

This section is one of the strongest conversion tools on the homepage.

### It should communicate
- NPI has real project experience
- the experience is relevant
- visitors can explore more

### Section content
- concise section heading
- short intro line
- 3 featured references max
- one CTA to view all references

### Reference card copy rule
Each card should privilege:
- project title
- location / client / year as relevant
- one short descriptor

Do not overcrowd reference cards with dense metadata.

### Intro text direction
The intro line should gently guide visitors toward:
- evidence
- range
- seriousness

Possible strategic direction:
Selected references that reflect the breadth and depth of NPI’s project experience.

Not final copy — just direction.

---

## H. Clients / Partners strip

### Section job
Support trust without becoming the main story.

### Copy strategy
This section needs very little text.
Often a small label or no paragraph at all is enough.

Possible label directions:
- Trusted by clients and partners
- Selected clients and project partners

Keep this restrained.

---

## I. Contact CTA section

### Section job
Give the visitor a clear next action.

### This section should communicate
- we are reachable
- the next step is simple
- contact is welcomed, not pressured

### Preferred homepage approach
Use a compact CTA block rather than a full heavy contact form.

Possible content:
- one heading
- one short paragraph
- one clear CTA button

### Message direction
The CTA should feel calm and professional.

Good direction:
- discuss your project with our team
- get in touch about your project needs
- connect with NPI

Avoid:
- aggressive conversion language
- fake urgency
- overselling

The Contact page can hold the fuller form experience.

---

## J. Footer

### Footer job
Provide structure and utility, not extra marketing.

### Footer content strategy
Use it for:
- short company line
- navigation
- services/quick links
- contact information
- legal links

### Footer summary text rule
One short summary sentence is enough.
Do not repeat the whole homepage message again.

---

## 6. Copy density rules by section

### Hero
- 1 headline
- 1 short paragraph
- 1 CTA
- optional short trust line

### About
- 1 heading
- 1 paragraph
- 1 short supporting proof detail

### Services
- 4–5 titles
- 4–5 one-sentence descriptions

### Process
- 1 heading
- 1 paragraph
- optional compact stage list

### Numbers
- 3–4 stat labels only

### Featured references
- short heading
- short intro
- 3 cards max

### Contact CTA
- 1 heading
- 1 short paragraph
- 1 CTA

If any section starts to feel text-heavy, it should be simplified.

---

## 7. Content paths the homepage should create

The homepage should gently push the visitor into one of three paths:

### Path 1 — “I want to understand the company”
Hero → About → Services → Process

### Path 2 — “I want proof”
Hero → Numbers → Featured References → All References

### Path 3 — “I may want to contact them”
Hero / About / References → Contact CTA → Contact page

This means each section should support at least one of these paths clearly.

---

## 8. What the homepage should deliberately avoid

Do not include:
- long dense paragraphs
- repetitive statements
- too many CTAs
- generic claims without evidence
- bloated technical detail
- all services and all sectors in one place
- brochure-style copy pasted directly into sections

The homepage should open doors.
It should not act as the entire archive of company information.

---

## 9. Copy-writing checklist for later drafting

When writing actual homepage copy, each section draft should be tested against these questions:

### Hero
- Does it sound like engineering, not generic business?
- Is it clear in under 5 seconds?

### About
- Does it explain who NPI is without turning into history?

### Services
- Can each service card be understood instantly?

### Process
- Does it explain how NPI works, not just what it claims?

### Numbers
- Are the proof points factual and defensible?

### References
- Does it make the visitor want to explore actual projects?

### CTA
- Is the next step clear and easy?

If the answer is “no,” the copy is not ready.

The homepage should not persuade by volume.

It should persuade by:
- clarity
- relevance
- sequence
- evidence
- restraint

If the copy does that well, the design will feel much stronger and much more specific to NPI.
