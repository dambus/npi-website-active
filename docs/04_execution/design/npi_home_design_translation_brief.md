# NPI Website — Home Design Translation Brief  
**Reference inspiration:** Bexon `index-4` homepage  
**Purpose:** Translate the visual logic of the reference template into a credible, modern, NPI-specific homepage direction without copying generic agency content or structure blindly.

---

## 1. Core principle

The goal is **not** to recreate the Bexon homepage literally.

The goal is to take what works from it:
- strong section rhythm
- premium spacing
- large-scale typography
- dark/light contrast between sections
- controlled animation
- elegant cards and split layouts
- contemporary corporate composition

…and translate that into a homepage that feels:
- engineering-first
- credible
- professional
- clear
- internationally presentable
- restrained, not boastful
- useful for investors, partners, and clients

The homepage should not feel like:
- a startup agency
- a generic business consulting site
- a marketing-heavy “we do everything” presentation
- a long brochure pasted into a web page

The homepage should feel like:
- a serious engineering company
- a technically capable project partner
- a company that can be trusted with complex industrial work
- a business that knows how to communicate clearly and confidently

---

## 2. Content philosophy

This is one of the most important decisions for the whole website.

### Rule 1 — Less, but better
The homepage should not try to say everything.

It should:
- establish trust
- explain what NPI does
- show how NPI works
- highlight selected references
- make the next action obvious

### Rule 2 — Every section must earn its place
If a section does not help the visitor:
- understand the company
- trust the company
- find a relevant service
- see relevant references
- contact the company

…it should not be on the homepage.

### Rule 3 — The homepage is not the brochure
The brochure can be longer and denser.

The homepage must be:
- lighter
- more direct
- easier to scan
- stronger in hierarchy
- more selective with information

### Rule 4 — NPI-specific messaging
The copy must sound like NPI.

That means:
- clear
- professional
- technically literate
- confident without exaggeration
- oriented toward real project work
- suitable for foreign and domestic stakeholders

Avoid:
- hype language
- generic business clichés
- empty “innovation / synergy / growth” phrases without substance
- overexplaining

---

## 3. Who the homepage is for

Primary visitors:
- investors
- project owners
- domestic and foreign clients
- partners
- EPC / industrial stakeholders

Secondary visitors:
- institutions
- potential employees
- general corporate audience

This means the homepage should quickly answer:

1. Who is NPI?  
2. What does NPI do?  
3. What types of projects does NPI support?  
4. Why should we trust NPI?  
5. How can we explore references?  
6. How do we get in touch?

---

## 4. Visual translation from the reference template

## What we are keeping from Bexon

### A. Section rhythm
The Bexon homepage works because each section feels distinct, spacious, and intentional.

We should keep:
- large vertical spacing
- alternation between dark and light sections
- strong separation between blocks
- full-width background treatment
- visual breathing room

### B. Large typography
We should keep:
- large, strong hero headline
- generous section titles
- clean hierarchy
- short supporting text

### C. Box systems where they make sense
The services and numbers blocks work well because:
- they are large
- they are not crowded
- they are visually consistent
- they use iconography well

We should keep the idea of:
- structured box systems
- but not make every section a box grid

### D. Split-section layout
The horizontal two-part section is highly useful for NPI and should be adapted.

### E. Subtle animation
We should keep:
- soft entry animations
- subtle hover motion
- count-up animation for stats
- refined nav show/hide
- maybe very light hero parallax

---

## What we are changing

### A. Tone
Bexon feels like a premium business/agency template.

NPI must feel more:
- industrial
- technical
- engineering-focused
- mature
- precise

### B. Geometry
The reference uses rounded design language often.

For NPI:
- use sharper, more architectural geometry
- reduce roundness overall
- keep rounding only where it adds softness intentionally
- avoid “everything is pill-shaped”

### C. Visual assets
Bexon uses generic corporate lifestyle visuals.

NPI should use:
- project visuals
- industrial imagery
- engineering drawings/renders where appropriate
- relevant plant / process / infrastructure context
- controlled abstract graphics if needed

### D. CTA density
Bexon uses many visible CTA moments.

NPI should use fewer, clearer CTAs:
- explore services
- view references
- contact us

## 5. Homepage structure for NPI

This is the recommended homepage order.

### 1. Sticky navigation
Purpose:
- orient the visitor
- keep navigation accessible
- reinforce brand calmness and structure

Behavior:
- visible at top of page
- sticky
- hides on downward scroll after roughly one viewport
- reappears on upward scroll with smooth animation
- always visible near the top area of the page

Style:
- light background
- clean typography
- minimal clutter
- no phone number or heavy CTA in header
- logo left, nav centered/right

Suggested nav items:
- Home
- About
- Services
- References
- Contact

---

### 2. Hero
Purpose:
- immediately communicate who NPI is and what kind of partner it is
- set the premium visual tone
- establish confidence and clarity

Content needs:
- short label/tag above the title
- large headline
- concise supporting copy
- primary CTA
- optional secondary trust line or short statement
- visual anchor below or within the hero

Message direction:
The hero should not sound generic.

It should clearly communicate:
- engineering capability
- project delivery support
- relevance to industrial/infrastructure sectors
- professionalism and confidence

Examples of message direction:
- engineering support for complex industrial projects
- integrated design and technical support
- from concept to execution in demanding environments
- trusted engineering partner for energy and industrial systems

Not final copy — only direction.

Visual direction:
- dark premium background
- subtle gradient / glow using NPI-related green tones
- possible very light parallax treatment
- large typography
- lots of negative space
- controlled compositional layering

---

### 3. Short About / Intro section
Purpose:
- quickly humanize and contextualize the company after the hero
- answer “Who are these people?” in a concise way
- transition from emotional first impression to factual credibility

Content needs:
- small label
- large but concise heading
- short paragraph
- small trust detail or mini stat
- partner/client logos slider or strip below

Message direction:
This section should frame NPI as:
- experienced
- multidisciplinary
- reliable
- active in relevant industries
- able to support projects from early concept to detailed execution

This is not the place for a long company history.

This is the place for:
- one clear company statement
- one reinforcing paragraph
- one or two credibility details

---

### 4. Key Services section
Purpose:
- help visitors quickly understand the company’s core service areas
- give the homepage functional utility
- present expertise clearly without overexplaining

Reference pattern:
The Bexon “solution boxes” are an excellent model.

NPI adaptation:
- 4 or 5 large service panels
- one central or leading text block
- large icons
- short descriptions
- minimal text per box
- elegant spacing

Possible service groups:
- Process & Mechanical Design
- Instrumentation & Control
- Electrical Engineering
- Telecommunications / Fire & Gas / Safety Systems
- Project Documentation & Engineering Support

This must be validated and refined later against real NPI service positioning.

Important:
Keep it short.
Each service block should feel scannable in 3–5 seconds.

---

### 5. How We Work / Project Lifecycle section
Purpose:
- communicate method, not just capability
- show that NPI supports projects through structured stages
- differentiate NPI from generic “service list” companies

Reference pattern:
The horizontal split section from the template should be adapted here.

Recommended NPI use:
**From concept to commissioning**  
or  
**How we support projects through each phase**

Layout:
- left: dark content block
- right: large project/engineering image or visual

Content direction:
This section should explain:
- studies / concept support
- permitting / technical documentation
- FEED / detailed engineering
- design coordination
- supervision / support during execution
- commissioning support where applicable

It should feel:
- practical
- process-oriented
- calm
- structured

This section can be one of the strongest on the page.

---

### 6. Numbers / Facts section
Purpose:
- create trust using factual signals
- provide quick corporate proof points
- reinforce scale without sounding boastful

Reference pattern:
The Bexon stats boxes are highly adaptable here.

Recommended NPI version:
**Neopetrol through numbers**  
or  
**NPI in numbers**

Likely data points:
- departments
- engineers
- completed projects
- returning clients
- years of experience if appropriate

Design direction:
- 3 or 4 large stat boxes
- light section background
- lots of whitespace
- strong number typography
- short labels only

Avoid:
- overly sales-like KPI language
- inflated or vague claims

---

### 7. Featured References
Purpose:
- make credibility concrete
- connect the visitor with real projects
- push toward the References section

This is one of the most important homepage sections.

Content needs:
- section title
- short intro
- 3 featured projects from Supabase
- clear CTA to all references

Reference direction:
Do not overload this area.
Three strong references are enough.

Each card should communicate:
- title
- location / client / year as relevant
- short descriptor
- image

The visual style should be stronger and more refined than the current implementation.
Less cramped.
More editorial.
More confidence.

---

### 8. Clients / Partners strip
Purpose:
- reinforce trust through association
- add visual rhythm
- show real business context

Can be:
- quiet slider
- marquee-like strip
- restrained logo row

Important:
This section should feel secondary, not louder than references.

---

### 9. Contact CTA section
Purpose:
- give the page a clear exit point
- make contact easy
- convert interested visitors without pressure

Reference direction:
The Bexon contact form styling is elegant and usable.

Recommended NPI approach:
Homepage should probably use either:
- a short contact form
or
- a compact CTA block with key contact information and one button

Preferred direction for now:
A compact CTA section is safer for the homepage.
The full form can live on Contact page.

However, visually we can still borrow the Bexon input/form atmosphere.

---

### 10. Footer
Purpose:
- conclude the page cleanly
- provide utility
- support navigation and corporate trust

Recommended content:
- short company summary
- services or quick links
- references / resources links
- contact details
- bottom legal line

Visual direction:
- calm
- structured
- column-based
- restrained bottom bar
- no visual noise

---

## 6. Content scope discipline by section

The homepage must remain concise.

### Hero
- 1 main heading
- 1 supporting paragraph
- 1 CTA
- 1 trust/supporting line max

### About intro
- 1 heading
- 1 paragraph
- 1 supporting detail
- logo strip

### Services
- 4–5 boxes
- 1 short text block
- very short descriptions

### Process
- 1 title
- 1 supporting text block
- optional 3-step or lifecycle note
- 1 image

### Numbers
- 3–4 stat boxes

### References
- 3 items max on homepage

### Clients/Partners
- 1 restrained strip

### Contact CTA
- 1 heading
- 1 short paragraph
- 1 CTA or short form

This discipline is important.
Too much information will damage the effect of the design.

## 7. Initial content messaging framework for NPI

This is not final copy.  
It is a content strategy direction for later writing.

### Core homepage message
NPI is a multidisciplinary engineering partner supporting industrial and infrastructure projects through structured technical expertise and real project experience.

### Supporting ideas to reinforce across the page
- integrated engineering support
- experience across demanding sectors
- clear technical delivery
- project lifecycle understanding
- practical, reliable documentation and design support
- references that demonstrate capability

### Things the copy should avoid
- generic innovation claims without specifics
- exaggerated self-praise
- long internal company descriptions
- unclear jargon without user value
- too many parallel messages in one section

### The homepage should gently guide the visitor toward:
1. understanding NPI quickly
2. exploring services
3. reviewing references
4. making contact

---

## 8. Animation direction

Yes, subtle animation should be used.

### Recommended
- fade-up on section entry
- slight stagger on service/stat/reference cards
- count-up effect on numbers
- soft reveal for logos/partner strip
- hover transitions on cards and CTAs
- smooth sticky nav hide/show
- very light hero parallax if performance remains clean

### Avoid
- dramatic floating
- overdone scaling
- long cinematic delays
- flashy motion for its own sake
- too many simultaneous animated elements

Animation should support:
- polish
- rhythm
- perception of quality

Not spectacle.

---

## 9. Color and styling direction

### Keep
- dark + light section alternation
- green-accent logic inspired by brand
- strong contrast
- clean UI

### Adjust for NPI
- less “soft agency teal”
- more controlled industrial green
- stronger neutrals
- fewer rounded edges
- sharper cards and panels
- more architectural spacing
- selective use of transparency/layering
- occasional irregular shapes only where meaningful

---

## 10. Immediate design decisions to lock before redesign

Before Codex starts the visual redesign, these decisions should be treated as fixed:

- homepage will follow the section order defined in this document
- copy must be NPI-specific, not generic business copy
- navigation will be light, sticky, and scroll-aware
- hero will be dark and premium
- services will use large structured panels
- process section will use split layout
- facts section will use large numeric blocks
- homepage references will stay selective
- contact CTA will remain restrained
- animations will stay subtle
- visual clutter must be reduced
- whitespace must increase compared to the current implementation
- overall public UI should move away from the current template-heavy feeling

---

## 11. Next content task — extremely important

Before or during visual implementation, the homepage copy should be written carefully section by section.

This should be done with the following rule:

**Every homepage sentence must help the visitor either understand, trust, explore, or contact NPI.**

That means we should next prepare:
- homepage copy outline
- section-by-section copy draft
- short vs long copy options where necessary
- language discipline rules

This step should be treated seriously.

The visual redesign will only become truly strong if the content is equally controlled.

## 13. Final note

This homepage should not try to impress by saying more.

It should impress by:
- saying the right things
- in the right order
- with the right amount of restraint
- inside a design system that feels clear, modern, and trustworthy

That is the standard the new NPI homepage should aim for.
