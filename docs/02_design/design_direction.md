# Design Direction

## Design Goals

- Present NPI as premium industrial, clean corporate, and technically credible.
- Keep the interface calm and structured so the work itself feels serious.
- Let references, facts, and delivery clarity create confidence instead of decorative marketing language.

## High-Level Reference Behavior

What to emulate from corporate template references at a high level:

- strong hierarchy in hero sections
- disciplined spacing and generous white space
- restrained use of accent color
- clean card systems for services and references
- image use that supports credibility rather than lifestyle branding

What to avoid:

- startup-style gradients and flashy motion
- overpacked pages with too many competing content blocks
- boastful copy, vague superlatives, and decorative statistics without context
- overly branded admin screens that slow practical CMS use

## Visual Tone

- Primary tone: premium industrial
- Secondary tone: clean corporate
- Trust signal: technical credibility

Homepage mood:

- assured, structured, and efficient
- dark-light contrast can be used to create emphasis, but not drama

References page mood:

- practical, clear, and data-forward
- content hierarchy should support scanning, filtering, and comparison

Admin UI mood:

- simple, practical, and low-friction
- utility should win over branding

## Color Direction

Brand usage based on current NPI cues:

- use deep neutral backgrounds for authority
- use warm amber/gold accents as a controlled highlight
- use white and warm stone neutrals for reading surfaces

Neutral supporting colors:

- warm off-white backgrounds
- medium stone grays for borders and quiet labels
- dark charcoal for text and structural contrast

## Typography Rules

- Headlines should feel firm and confident, not decorative.
- Body copy should prioritize clarity and moderate line length.
- Use uppercase sparingly for eyebrow labels, metadata, and navigation accents.
- Large statements should be short enough to scan in one pass.

## Spacing Philosophy

- Use generous section spacing to give the site a premium corporate rhythm.
- Keep internal spacing consistent so cards and text blocks feel engineered, not improvised.
- Avoid stacking too many small elements without a larger visual pause.

## Section Rhythm

- Alternate dense and breathable sections.
- Keep each section focused on one communication goal.
- Follow message flow: orientation, proof, capability, process, conversion.

## Component Rules

Card style for references and services:

- medium radius, clean border, subtle shadow
- concise titles and short supporting text
- metadata should feel structured, not noisy

Image usage rules:

- prefer project, equipment, process, and facility imagery over generic office visuals
- crop consistently and avoid decorative mosaics
- every image should have a communication role, not just fill space

Motion and animation rules:

- minimal and restrained
- prioritize fade, slight lift, and staggered entrance only where it helps readability
- no continuous motion and no attention-grabbing hover theatrics

## Content Discipline

- keep copy short, factual, and non-boastful
- write in a corporate, engineering-led tone
- prioritize evidence, scope, and delivery context over slogans
